What I Do

Broadly speaking, I use machine learning, Bayesian inference and physics-based modelling to answer a range of questions. For example:

  • Where is the Galactic Underworld, the Milky Way's population of black holes and neutron stars?
  • How can we efficiently search microlensing events to find it?
  • How do we best implement semi-supervised learning to take advantage of the flood of astronomical survey data?
  • Can we detect the greenwashing of emissions reported by companies and nations?
And when I'm not attempting to answer those questions, I'm working on translating a clever piece of photonics into a usable methane sensor for agriculture and waste management.

Teaching and Supervision

I have a long history of teaching computer science, data science and machine learning. As well as teaching a dozen units units, I've written tutorials ranging from introductory Python material, Bayesian inference and machine learning for honours-level Physicists to the capstone unit for the University of Sydney's Business Analytics masters program.

I currently supervise two PhD students, Stephanie Rossini-Bryson and Max Charles, who work on identifying exoplanets with a nulling interferometer and using automatic differentiation to reconstruct images from the AMI instrument aboard the James Webb Space Telescope, respectively.
